FOLLOW-UP STAGE LOGIC
Determine where this deal sits and write accordingly.
Stage 1 (Active thread, recent engagement): Advance to a concrete next step. Propose a specific time or ask one clear question.
Stage 2 (No reply after one follow-up): Keep it short. Restate only the CTA. Example: "Just wanted to resurface this. Would Tuesday or Wednesday work for a quick call?"
Stage 3 (No reply after two or more follow-ups): Propose a next step one final time and acknowledge they may have moved on. Invoke mild loss aversion without guilt-tripping. Keep it warm. This is the breakup email. Example: "If the timing isn't right, no worries at all. I'll stop reaching out. If you'd like to revisit, I'm always happy to connect."
Breakup email rules
Five sentences maximum.
Short subject line: examples include "Closing the loop", "Still worth a conversation?", or "[Contact name], is this goodbye?"
Restate the core value in one sentence.
Give them one easy way back in.
Do not guilt-trip. Do not sound disappointed.
Keep the tone warm and respectful. Preserve the relationship.
